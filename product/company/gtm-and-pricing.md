# GTM And Pricing

## GTM Thesis

Litmus should use a developer-led, team-expanded motion.

The distribution model is:

1. developer adopts Litmus in the local AI coding loop
2. team adds Litmus to pull request workflows
3. manager buys policy, merge control, and team-wide confidence visibility
4. larger organizations expand into platform and deploy controls

This is closer to the adoption path of products like Graphite than a top-down infrastructure sale, while still preserving a deeper technical moat underneath.

## Initial ICP

The initial ideal customer profile:

- AI-heavy engineering teams
- 5 to 100 engineers
- backend services with meaningful production risk
- high PR volume relative to review capacity
- engineering leaders who care about both velocity and incident prevention

Best verticals early:

- fintech
- developer tools
- B2B SaaS with integration-heavy backends
- marketplaces
- infra or automation products with service orchestration logic

## Entry Motion

### Product-Led Entry

Free or low-friction developer tier:

- local verify
- local replay
- limited MCP / agent usage
- limited PR checks on small repos or capped monthly usage

Goal:

- create memorable catches
- make Litmus part of the agent loop
- generate internal pull from engineers

### Team Conversion

Paid team offering should unlock:

- unlimited PR verification
- merge gates and policy controls
- shared run history and evidence
- service criticality settings
- team-level confidence reporting

### Sales Assist

Use light sales for:

- larger teams
- critical-path services
- multi-repo rollouts
- companies wanting deploy controls or incident-learning workflows

## Packaging

### Free Developer

- individual use
- local-only workflows
- capped PR checks or monthly verification budget
- basic replay artifacts

### Team

- priced per active engineer
- PR checks and merge policies
- shared control plane
- team evidence retention
- service-level settings

### Enterprise

- platform controls
- advanced policy
- longer retention
- dedicated support
- deployment and incident integrations

## Pricing Direction

Pricing should match value delivered, not raw compute.

Suggested starting point:

- Free Developer: $0
- Team: $40 to $60 per active engineer per month
- Enterprise: annual contract with platform minimums

Usage-based overages can exist later, but the first paid motion should stay easy to understand.

The team product should be sold on:

- faster safe merges
- fewer expensive regressions
- less wasted reviewer time
- better AI-code adoption confidence

## Core GTM Message

The message should be simple:

**"AI can write the code. Litmus tells you if it is safe to ship."**

Secondary proof points:

- catches failures tests and review miss
- gives agents replayable evidence, not vague feedback
- turns incident patterns into future merge protection

## GTM Risks

- pitching the product as generic testing infra
- sounding like a broad governance suite too early
- requiring a heavy setup motion before the first value moment
- hiding unsupported gaps and weakening trust

## GTM Outcome

If the motion works, Litmus becomes:

- loved by developers because it catches real issues
- bought by managers because it improves team velocity with less fear
- expanded by platform teams because it becomes part of shipping policy
